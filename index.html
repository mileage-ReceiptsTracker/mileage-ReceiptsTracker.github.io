What This App Does

Mileage & Receipts Tracker uses iPhone GPS to automatically detect and record driving trips. Classify each trip with a single gesture using the Track & Swipe method — swipe right for business, left for personal. The app calculates tax deductions using the Smart CRA Split (automatic CRA two-tier rates: $0.72/km for the first 5,000 km, $0.66/km after) and the IRS standard mileage rate ($0.70/mile).

Attach parking receipts directly to any trip using Trip Card Receipts — the built-in OCR scanner automatically extracts the receipt amount. View your mileage across regions with the Province Breakdown, and never lose a route in dead zones thanks to Gap Fill Recovery, which stitches offline routes using road network data.

The app follows a Free-to-Track model: you see all your mileage data, classify trips, and view summaries for free. You only pay when you need to export reports for your accountant.

How It Works — The Track & Swipe Method

Step 1: Track

Turn on the app and drive. iPhone GPS and motion sensors automatically detect every trip — start location, end location, route, distance, and timestamps. No buttons to press, no manual logging. Even in dead zones with no cell signal, Gap Fill Recovery reconstructs the missing route segments using road network data after you reconnect.

Step 2: Swipe

At the end of your day, open the app and swipe each trip: right for business, left for personal. That's the Track & Swipe method. If you set up a work schedule, the app can auto-classify trips during work hours — no swiping needed.

Step 3: Smart CRA Split (or IRS Rate)

For Canadian users, the Smart CRA Split automatically applies the CRA two-tier rate: $0.72/km for the first 5,000 business kilometers, $0.66/km for each kilometer after. No spreadsheet formulas, no manual tier tracking. The app handles the split automatically as your annual kilometers accumulate. US users get the IRS standard rate of $0.70/mile applied automatically.

Step 4: Trip Card Receipts

Paid for parking during a trip? Open the trip card, snap a photo of the receipt. The Trip Card Receipts feature uses OCR to automatically extract the dollar amount. The receipt is attached to that specific trip and included in your exported reports.

Step 5: Province Breakdown & Export

The Province Breakdown shows exactly how many kilometers you drove in each Canadian province or US state — essential for multi-region contractors. When tax season arrives, export a professional PDF or CSV report with one tap. The Free-to-Track model means you see all your data for free; you only pay when you export.

Yes. The app auto-detects whether you are in Canada or the United States and applies the correct tax authority automatically. For 2026, the IRS standard mileage rate is $0.70 per mile. The app uses the Track & Swipe method: iPhone GPS and motion sensors automatically detect when you start and stop driving. Trips are recorded with start and end locations, distance, route map, and timestamps — no manual input required. Short consecutive trips with stops under 5 minutes are automatically joined. The app works in the background using battery-efficient location services.

Can I classify trips as business or personal?

Yes — that's the Track & Swipe method. Swipe right to classify a trip as business, left for personal.
